Plataforma

A cooperative network for discovering and cataloging creative work — built with and for artists.

Plataforma is creating an online directory where artists are discoverable by curators, collectors, fellow artists, and the public. Not a marketplace. Not a social network. A structured, searchable, browsable map of creative production across disciplines, built and governed by the artists themselves.

Founded by Katie Dixon and Okwui Okpokwasili. NYC-anchored. Currently in scoping phase.

The idea

Artists are hard to find. A personal portfolio site is discoverable only if someone already knows the artist's name. Gallery websites showcase their own roster. Existing platforms are marketplaces first — discovery is a byproduct of selling, not a goal in itself.

Meanwhile, the information that would make discovery possible — structured records of who made what, where it has been shown, which collections hold it — exists but is scattered across museum databases, gallery spreadsheets, Wikidata entries, and CV PDFs.

Plataforma assembles this information into a single, navigable, artist-controlled directory. When an artist joins, the platform searches public databases for works already attributed to them. The experience is "we found 47 of your works in museum collections — confirm?" rather than "please enter your first artwork."

Approach

Cohorts or "Pods" of artists join Plataforma as a group with one or two leaders who are early adopters. Pods are built around existing connections between artists and their interest in sharing those collaborations publicly. Starting with New York City artists and institutions for the initial build phase, then expanding along the international networks of participating NYC artists.
