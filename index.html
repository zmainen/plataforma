Plataforma

A cooperative network for discovering and cataloging creative work — built with and for artists.

↓ Scroll

Artists are invisible online

A personal portfolio site is discoverable only if someone already knows your name. Galleries showcase their own roster. Existing platforms are marketplaces first — discovery is a byproduct of selling, not a goal.

Meanwhile, AI threatens ownership, public grants are being divested, and social media demands that artists game algorithms in the hope of finding financial benefactors. The information that would make discovery possible — structured records of who made what, where it has been shown, which collections hold it — exists but is scattered across museum databases, gallery spreadsheets, and CV PDFs.

How it works

01

We find your work for you

When you join, Plataforma searches the collections of major museums, Wikidata, and public databases for works already attributed to you. The experience is "we found 47 of your works in museum collections — confirm?" not "please enter your first artwork."

02

Your images, your control

Museums release metadata but restrict images of contemporary work. Your career history auto-populates from public sources, but the visual representation of your work comes from you — because only you should control it.

03

An ORCID for artists

Scientists have ORCID. Artists have nothing comparable. Plataforma links your profile to every existing identifier — Wikidata, Getty ULAN, ISNI, MusicBrainz — and gives you one persistent, portable URI across all systems.

04

Every discipline

Not just visual art. A painting has dimensions; a composition has duration; a film has cast. The catalog handles creative work across media — one artist, one catalog, every discipline.

What's different

You own your data

Revocable license. Full export anytime. If you leave, your data is deleted — not archived.

No training without consent

Any intelligence built from artist data requires explicit, granular, revocable opt-in.

Artists govern

Plataforma is a cooperative. Members set the policies, not investors.

Value flows to you

Revenue from your data — recommendations, licensing, sales — is tracked and distributed to contributors.

These are structural commitments, not terms-of-service promises. Read the founders' concept →

Growing through Pods

Artists join Plataforma as a group — cohorts built around existing connections, with one or two early-adopter leaders. Starting in New York City, expanding along the international networks of participating artists.

The first pods will be drawn from the founders' personal networks, starting with Okwui Okpokwasili and the Sweat Variant team. Institutions, galleries, and fans follow the artists in.
